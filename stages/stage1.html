stage 1 tumors

what is stage 1

Stage 1 tumors are the earliest stage of true cancer. These tumors are small and if they have spread at all to lymph nodes, the spread is only microscopic. The cancer is small and has only spread a little into nearby tissues. It has not spread to any lymph nodes or other body areas.

symptoms of stage 1 tumors

The symptoms of a brain tumor depend on its size and location in the brain. Some slow-growing tumors may not cause any symptoms at first. Eventually, the tumor may put pressure on the brain, which may cause signs and symptoms such as severe headaches that happen more often and seem more severe, seizures, sensory changes such vision, smell, and hearing, nausea or vomiting.

cure and prevention

The treatment options for stage 1 tumors depend on the type and location of the cancer. In general, the main treatment options for stage 1 tumors include: Surgery: This is often the main treatment for stage 1 tumors. The goal of surgery is to remove the tumor and a margin of surrounding healthy tissue. In some cases, surgery may be the only treatment necessary, particularly if the cancer is small and has not spread to nearby lymph nodes. Radiation therapy: This treatment uses high-energy radiation to kill cancer cells. It may be used in combination with surgery or as the primary treatment for some types of cancer. Chemotherapy: This treatment uses drugs to kill cancer cells. It may be used in combination with surgery or radiation therapy or as the primary treatment for some types of cancer. Immunotherapy: This treatment works by stimulating the body's immune system to attack cancer cells. It may be used in combination with other treatments or as a stand-alone therapy.
